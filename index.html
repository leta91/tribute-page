Saint Pope John Paul II

A man who changed the world

The key points of his extraordinary life:

  • 1920: Karol Józef Wojtyła was born in the Polish town of Wadowice
  • 1932: His older brother Edmund was a doctor and, while treating a patient with scarlet fever, lost his life and this impacted the young Karol deeply
  • 1942: He decided to study for the priesthood. He began courses in the clandestine underground seminary run by the Archbishop of Kraków
  • During the World War II he helped saving the lives of many Jewish people
  • 1946: He was ordained a preist on All Saints' Day
  • 1947: During his sojourn at the Angelicum, Karol visited Padre Pio, who heard his confession and told him that one day he would ascend to "the highest post in the Church". They established a strong, lasting bond based on their common religious beliefs
  • 1978: Karol became Pope of the Catholic Church
  • As an extension of his successful work with youth as a young priest, Pope John Paul II pioneered the international World Youth Days. He always put great attention to the young people as they are the future
  • He spoke with courage against wars and violence that turns humans into beasts and made important conversations with other religious leaders
  • 1981: He forgave his attempted murderer
  • He made public apologies for over 100 wrongdoings of the Catholic Church, including: the crusades, the inactivity and silence of many Catholics during the Holocaust, its involvement in the African slave trade, the abuses made by some that used their belonging to the Church as a way to abuse trusting kids and people of all ages
  • 2005: He died in Rome
  • Many miracles have been recognized, after having scientifically studied each medical case, that occurred through the intercession of Pope John Paul II. These miracles will lead to him being declared a Saint
  • 2011: The beatification of Pope John Paul II
  • 2014: The canonisation of Pope John Paul II and Pope John XXIII
